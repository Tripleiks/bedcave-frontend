## Titanium: Not Just Marketing Fluff

Let's talk about the frame material for a moment, because it genuinely matters. Most folding bikes use aluminum or steel. Aluminum is light but can fatigue and crack over time. Steel is durable but heavy. Titanium is the sweet spot:

| Material | Weight | Durability | Corrosion Resistance | Ride Feel |
|----------|--------|------------|----------------------|-----------|
| Steel | Heavy | Excellent | Moderate | Stiff |
| Aluminum | Light | Good | Good | Harsh |
| **Titanium** | **Light** | **Excellent** | **Excellent** | **Smooth** |

Titanium has a natural flex that absorbs road vibration, giving you a more comfortable ride over rough urban surfaces. It's also essentially immune to rust and corrosion, which means your investment stays in pristine condition for years — even if you're riding through rain, road salt, or the general chaos of city streets.

## Hydraulic Disc Brakes: Because Stopping Matters Too

It might be easy to focus on all the "go" features, but how a bike *stops* is equally critical in city traffic. The Vello Automatic+ ships with **hydraulic disc brakes** on both wheels — the same technology used on high-end mountain bikes and road bikes.

Hydraulic disc brakes offer:
- **Consistent stopping power** in wet or dry conditions
- **Self-adjusting pads** that compensate for wear
- **Light lever feel** — no death grip required
- **Superior heat dissipation** for long descents or repeated braking

In a city where a taxi might cut you off at any moment, having brakes that respond instantly and predictably isn't a luxury — it's a necessity.
